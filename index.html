Heart Attack

A Common Silent Killer

A heart attack (also known as myocardial infarction) happens when a part of the heart muscle does not get enough blood. This is due to sudden obstruction of blood flow in the coronary artery. Part of the heart muscle dies in the process due to oxygen deprivation, giving rise to crushing chest pain.

    Symptoms
  • Chest Pain or Palpitations
  • Shortness of Breath
  • Cold Sweat
  • Giddiness
  • Heartburn/Indigestion
  • Nausea
